# AI Ethical Futures Lab Visual Style Guide

## Overview

AI Ethical Futures Lab is BC + AI's civic policy lab for public-interest AI:
facilitated rooms, human-synthesized community input, practical tools, policy
monitoring, and public outputs. The visual system should feel like the working
room where policy becomes practice.

Use this style for AEFL event covers, Luma thumbnails, social graphics, policy
cards, and review boards where the image needs to feel human, rigorous,
accountable, warm, and beautiful.

## Core Language

- Civic field notes, not tech conference branding
- Working-room proof: worksheets, laptops, table light, facilitation marks,
  public-interest artifacts, and careful synthesis
- Human values: care, trust, accountability, community, shared understanding,
  and room boundaries
- Thread and woven-material metaphors for holding tensions together
- Matte dark editorial surfaces with soft light, not glossy SaaS gradients
- BC + AI identity generated as part of the artwork, never pasted on like a
  sticker

## Palette

- Near-black ink, black-green, and deep blue-violet grounds
- Warm amber, coral, and soft paper-gold for care and room light
- Coast teal, mint, and acid-lime as restrained BC + AI accents
- Birch white or warm paper-white for readable typography
- Use saturation sparingly; the style should feel alive, not loud

## Composition

- One clear idea per image
- Strong readable title zone and stable lower logo/tagline zone
- Generous negative space around type and identity
- Prefer quiet artifact compositions: a thread grid, a luminous care loop,
  handwritten field-note marks, soft table-light, a paper stack, a public
  question held in space, or a simple woven civic object
- If a relationship is shown, make it sparse and hand-held: a few lines, one
  loop, or a woven crossing. Never build a dense web.

## Texture

- Premium matte poster finish
- Subtle paper tooth, pencil softness, thread fiber, warm glow, and gentle
  atmospheric gradients
- Clean enough for public partner-facing graphics
- No dirty grunge, rot, heavy photocopy distress, or messy texture

## Typography

- Crisp editorial sans-serif, large and confident for the AEFL title
- Small invitation line and bottom tagline should remain clean and legible
- Text should feel designed into the poster, not overlaid afterward
- Do not invent extra readable text beyond what the prompt requests

## Hard Bans

- Circuit boards, PCB traces, chip diagrams, electronic schematics
- Dense dot-line webs, node maps, constellation networks, mycelial maps
- Credential boards, certification graphics, badges, seals, medallions, emblems
- Generic AI stock visuals, robots, glowing brains, dashboards, fake UI
- Fake sponsor marks, fake venue claims, fake dates, fake URLs
- Campfires, canoes, faux-Indigenous motifs, ritual scenes, culture cosplay
- Corporate hype, sterile boardroom imagery, dirty grunge, clutter

## Example Prompt Add-On

```text
Render this in the AI Ethical Futures Lab style: a civic policy lab poster that
feels like the working room where policy becomes practice. Use matte dark
editorial surfaces, warm paper-light, sparse thread or field-note metaphors,
careful human synthesis, public-interest rigor, and restrained BC + AI teal,
coral, mint, and acid-lime accents. Keep one clear visual idea, generous
negative space, crisp typography, and a native generative BC+AI ecosystem mark.
Absolutely avoid circuits, PCB traces, dense node maps, credentials, badges,
seals, generic AI stock, fake UI, fake sponsors, campfires, canoes,
faux-Indigenous motifs, dirty grunge, and extra readable text.
```
