# BC AI Ecosystem Visual Style Guide

## Overview

BC AI Ecosystem is the polished, logo-forward identity direction for BC + AI assets. It borrows the living-network energy from the Futureproof experiments, but keeps the hierarchy clean enough for partner-facing materials, credentials, badges, schedules, sponsor cards, and ecosystem diagrams.

Use it when the BC + AI identity needs to be legible, central, and reusable.

## Core Language

- BC + AI mark as an embedded system node, not a sticker
- Circular badges, credentials, track emblems, mandalas, network maps, and campaign templates
- DNA rings, eye-garden mandalas, mycelial routes, organic circuits, heartbeat horizons, and luminous gateways
- BC nature as civic infrastructure: cedar, coastline, Salish Sea, Vancouver skyline, forest networks, salmon routes, ravens, orcas
- A premium community artifact feel: collectible, trustworthy, alive

## Palette

- Deep forest green and blackened evergreen
- Coast teal and aurora cyan
- Warm gold and birch white
- Restrained coral accents for warmth and attention
- Use high contrast when text or logos appear

## Composition

- Clear hierarchy with generous margins
- Logo, title, date, track, or partner name must remain readable
- Radial and network layouts are preferred over rigid hierarchy
- Badge stacks, credentials, stickers, schedules, speaker cards, and sponsor thank-yous should feel like one coherent system
- More presentation-ready than Futureproof Mythic, but still organic

## Texture

- Subtle glow, print grain, premium matte surfaces, layered paper or enamel-badge depth
- Clean enough for production use
- Organic connection lines and soft luminous edges

## Use For

- BC + AI ecosystem visuals
- Event credentials, speaker cards, sponsor thank-yous, stickers, and track badges
- Network diagrams and ecosystem maps
- Partner-facing social assets
- Futureproof assets where the BC + AI identity is the hero

## Avoid

- Mystical clutter that hides the logo
- Generic tech-conference gradients
- Sterile dashboards, rigid flowcharts, and stock SaaS illustration
- Overly literal PNW tourism imagery without the ecosystem/network layer
