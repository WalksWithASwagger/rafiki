# BC AI Community Centre Visual Style Guide

## Overview

The BC AI Community Centre visual identity is built on the concept of the **mycelial network** – nature's original decentralized system. Just as mycelium connects an entire forest underground through intricate, resilient pathways, BC AI represents the interconnected nature of our community-driven AI ecosystem.

This style bridges the organic beauty of BC's natural environment with the innovation of artificial intelligence, creating a professional yet rooted aesthetic.

## Core Design Philosophy

Our visuals embody three core principles:

1. **Organic over mechanical** - Natural, flowing forms instead of rigid structures
2. **Connection over isolation** - Emphasizing relationships and networks
3. **Growth over stasis** - Dynamic, evolving systems

## Color Palette

### Primary Colors

- **Deep Forest Green**: `#1E3A2B`
  - Represents BC's natural environment and sustainability
  - Use for main structural elements, primary nodes

- **Coast Blue**: `#175E7C`
  - Symbolizes innovation and the Pacific connection
  - Use for connections, flow indicators, secondary nodes

- **Earth Brown**: `#78552B`
  - Grounding, representing the mycelial roots
  - Use for foundational elements, base layers

### Secondary Colors

- **Birch White**: `#F7F4E9`
  - For clean space and contrast
  - Use for backgrounds, breathing room

- **Twilight Purple**: `#523E7B`
  - Used sparingly for areas of focus
  - Use for highlighting key concepts, special emphasis

- **Sunrise Orange**: `#D35C37`
  - Accent color for highlighting key elements
  - Use for CTAs, important callouts, energy indicators

### Functional Colors

- **Lichen Gray**: `#D9DBD7`
  - For secondary text and background elements
  - Use for supporting information, de-emphasized content

- **Growth Green**: `#68B091`
  - For positive indicators and interactive elements
  - Use for active states, progress, growth metrics

- **Warning Amber**: `#DFAA5B`
  - For cautionary elements and temporary states
  - Use sparingly for alerts, attention-getters

## Typography

### Font Stack

- **Headings**: Montserrat Bold
  - Clean, contemporary sans-serif that reflects confidence
  - Use for titles, headlines, major section dividers

- **Body Text**: Open Sans
  - Excellent readability across digital and print
  - Use for all body text, captions, annotations

- **Technical Labeling**: Source Code Pro
  - Monospaced font for technical annotations
  - Use for code examples, technical specifications

### Font Sizing

Follow a 1.5 scale ratio from base body text (16px):
- Body: 16px
- Small: 12px
- Large: 24px
- Heading 3: 36px
- Heading 2: 54px
- Heading 1: 80px

## Visual Elements

### The Mycelial Network Metaphor

The primary visual motif across all materials:

**Characteristics:**
- Organic, branching connections (not straight lines)
- Nodes of varying sizes at connection points
- Subtle gradients flowing along connection paths
- Irregular, natural growth patterns (avoid perfect symmetry)

**Implementation:**
- Connections should have subtle glowing edges
- Use semi-transparent gradients for depth
- Vary intensity to show activity/importance
- Branch paths organically, following natural curves

### Node Styling

**Visual Treatment:**
- Circular with soft edges
- Internal radial gradients (center to edge)
- Light drop shadows for depth (not harsh)
- Size variation indicates importance/centrality
- Text labels positioned outside when possible

**States:**
- Active: Brighter, more saturated
- Inactive: Muted, lower opacity
- Highlighted: Glowing edge, elevated

### Connection Lines

**Visual Treatment:**
- Organic curves, not straight or perfectly bezier
- Varying thickness indicates strength/importance
- Subtle glow effect suggests energy flow
- Bidirectional flow where appropriate
- May braid or intertwine where paths converge

## Diagram Specifications

### Ecosystem Diagrams

**Layout Principles:**
- **Central Hub**: Often represents "Ethics, Compliance, Standards, and Social License"
- **Connection Style**: Organic, branch-like paths rather than straight lines
- **Layout Options**:
  - Radial from center (hub-and-spoke with organic curves)
  - Distributed network (no single center, emphasizes decentralization)
  - **Never** purely hierarchical top-down
- **White Space**: Minimum 15% of canvas should be white space

**Content Hierarchy:**
- Primary concepts: Larger nodes, bolder connections
- Secondary concepts: Medium nodes, standard connections
- Tertiary concepts: Smaller nodes, lighter connections

### Technical Diagrams

**Layout Principles:**
- Subtle underlying grid for alignment (but don't show it)
- Connection flow shows bidirectional relationships
- Layering uses slight shadow/elevation for hierarchy
- Always include clear legend for specialized notation

## Photographic Style

When using photography:

- **Prioritize**: Local BC environments and communities
- **Representation**: Diverse creators, technologists, community members
- **Lighting**: Natural lighting preferred
- **Scale**: Both macro (close detail) and wide contextual shots
- **Authenticity**: Authentic moments over posed/corporate photography

## Animation Principles

For dynamic/interactive presentations:

- **Growth Animation**: Elements grow organically from connection points
- **Flow Animation**: Subtle particle effects along connection paths
- **Timing**: Natural, eased timing (never linear/mechanical)
- **Perspective**: Occasional subtle parallax effects for depth

## Design Don'ts

Avoid these elements:

- Corporate-looking grids and rigid hierarchical structures
- Perfect geometrical shapes that appear mechanical
- Clip art or generic stock illustrations
- Imagery that references surveillance or control
- Clichéd AI visuals (robots, brain circuits, glowing heads)
- Overly technical schematics without human context
- Straight connection lines without organic curves

## Application Examples

### Slide Presentations

- Use mycelial network as consistent navigation metaphor
- Incorporate subtle animation showing connection growth
- Reserve deep backgrounds for key conceptual slides
- Use lighter backgrounds for content-heavy slides

### Web Interfaces

- Interactive elements respond with subtle growth/expansion
- Navigation reveals connections between content areas
- Mobile interfaces use vertical mycelial flows
- Hover states show glowing edges

### Print Materials

- Create depth through layered mycelial patterns
- Highlight key content through node-like focal points
- Use cover designs suggesting emergent growth from edges
- Maintain sufficient white space for breathing room

## Accessibility Considerations

- Maintain minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio for all text
- Avoid conveying information through color alone
- Provide alternative descriptions for complex visual diagrams
- Test designs with color blindness simulators
- Ensure animations can be disabled for users with vestibular disorders

## When to Use BC AI Style

Use the BC AI Community Centre style for:

- **Ecosystem Diagrams**: Visualizing BC AI as a connected network
- **Community Content**: Showcasing collaborative, networked initiatives
- **Stakeholder Maps**: Showing relationships between partners and participants
- **Infrastructure Visualizations**: Data flows, compute resources, talent networks
- **Professional Presentations**: Board meetings, investor updates, partner pitches
- **Marketing Materials**: When representing the BC AI Community Centre brand

## When NOT to Use BC AI Style

Avoid this style for:

- Personal blog content (use HOPECODE instead)
- Anti-establishment or radical perspectives (use HOPECODE)
- Highly technical documentation requiring precision over metaphor (use kk)
- Content that needs dark, modern tech aesthetic (use kk)

## Comparison with Other Styles

**vs. kk (BC + AI personal brand):**
- BC AI: Organic, natural colors, mycelial networks, BC environment focus
- kk: Dark tech, teal/purple, modern editorial, more corporate

**vs. HOPECODE:**
- BC AI: Professional-organic, structured yet natural, polished
- HOPECODE: Anti-corporate, hand-drawn, messier, more radical

**Positioning:**
BC AI style sits between the polished professionalism of kk and the organic radicalism of HOPECODE - it's "rainforest meets innovation."

## Implementation

Use BC AI style with:

```bash
npx image-gen --prompt "your prompt" --style bcai
```

The style suffix automatically applies:
- Mycelial network aesthetic
- BC natural color palette
- Organic connections with glowing edges
- Professional yet rooted in nature
