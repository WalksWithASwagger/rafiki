# HOPECODE Visual Style Guide

> **Solarpunk Mycelial Mapping.** HOPECODE is not branding. It's a **cultural protocol**. A graphic language for mapping where we're going—without selling our souls to the cloud. It's the opposite of scale-for-scale's-sake. It's **root-first, not reach-first**.

## Core Aesthetic

HOPECODE is an infographic and diagram style designed for rebels, thinkers, artists, and builders who believe the future should feel like something other than a spreadsheet.

Structure as story. Data as ecology. Diagrams that **breathe**, not conform.

This isn't UI. This is a ritual—a way to **see the invisible connections** under empire, under asphalt, under code.

## Visual Principles

### Linework
- **Jittered, Human, Non-Euclidean**: Forget gridlines—**follow root lines**
- **Organic Forms**: Arrows, braids, spirals—forms that grow, not control
- **Meandering Paths**: Straight lines lie. Use paths that **meander with intent**
- **Hand-drawn Quality**: Embrace imperfection and human touch

### Color Palette

**Earth Tones + Spectral Interference:**
- **Earth tones**: ochre, ash, rust, moss
- **Spectral interference**: iridescent oil slick, electric fungi glow
- **No hex codes. No palette compliance.**
- Color is not identity—it's **atmosphere, weather, mood**

Think: Autumn forest floor meets bioluminescent cave system.

### Texture

- **Leave the mess in**: Paper grain, analog bleed, archival rot, photocopy scars
- Texture isn't decoration—it's **evidence of time**
- Diagrams should **look found, not made**
- Embrace imperfections: coffee stains, folds, xerox artifacts

### Typography

- **Code font crossbred with field notes**: Monospace + marginalia
- **Two voices in tension**: One for structure. One for soul. Both should **disagree in beautiful ways**
- **Annotations over hierarchy**: Meaning lives in the **footnotes, sidebars, and scraps**
- Handwritten notes in the margins
- Fragments, asides, whispers

## Tone & Philosophy

- **Irreverent**: Sacred doesn't mean precious. Call bullshit when you see it
- **Embodied**: These aren't "ideas." They're breath, skin, land, history
- **Visionary**: Not utopia. Not apocalypse. Just **something else, finally**
- **Anti-colonial**: Burn the blueprints. Compost the charts. Make new maps

## Structure Elements

### Nodes
People, places, values, moments—**not just data**
- Organic circular forms
- Varied sizes suggesting importance and connection
- Labels that feel handwritten or found

### Branches
Non-linear relationships, ancestral lines, tangents
- Meandering connections that show how things actually relate
- Not straight arrows—curved, organic paths
- Braided or intertwined where appropriate

### Zones
Contexts, vibes, ecosystems—**not boxes**
- Atmospheric regions with soft boundaries
- Color as mood, not category
- Overlapping and bleeding into each other

### Glyphs
Symbols with layered meaning—open source and sacred
- Icons that carry multiple interpretations
- Hand-drawn or found symbols
- Cultural symbols used with respect and context

### Legends
Minimal. Use when needed. Let people **feel it first**.

## Function

HOPECODE diagrams are tools for:

- **Collective clarity** without centralization
- **Mapping the mess** instead of erasing it
- **Reclaiming imagination** from empire's aesthetics

They're made to:

- Be **forked**, **remixed**, **misused**, **ritualized**
- Show up in **Notion dashboards**, **AR layers**, **city walls**, **zines**, **murals**, **tattoos**
- Evolve with context. Never be final.

## When to Use HOPECODE

Use this style for:

- **Personal Blog Content**: Your own voice, your own journey
- **Thought Leadership**: Anti-establishment, radical, visionary perspectives
- **Community Mapping**: Relationship networks, grassroots organizing
- **Alternative Futures**: Speculative design, solarpunk visions
- **Systems Thinking**: Complexity that resists reduction
- **Journey Maps**: Personal transformation, non-linear paths
- **Concept Webs**: Ideas that refuse categorization

## When NOT to Use HOPECODE

Avoid this style for:
- Corporate presentations (use KK/BC+AI style)
- Formal business proposals
- Technical documentation requiring precision
- Contexts requiring institutional credibility

## Implementation

Use HOPECODE style with:
```bash
npx image-gen --prompt "your prompt" --style hopecode
```

The style suffix is defined in `styles/styles.yaml` and emphasizes:
- Solarpunk mycelial mapping aesthetic
- Jittered, hand-drawn linework
- Earth tones with spectral interference
- Analog texture and imperfection
- Anti-corporate, found-not-made quality
