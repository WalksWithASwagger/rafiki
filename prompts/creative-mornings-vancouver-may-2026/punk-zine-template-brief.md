# Punk Zine Template Brief — Release Day (May 29, 2026)

Creative brief for the Release Day digital broadsheet collecting Creative Mornings audience projects.

---

## Concept

**Sniffin' Glue for 2026.**

Mark Perry printed 60 copies on a borrowed Xerox machine in 1976. We're doing the digital equivalent: a broadsheet collecting every project that Creative Mornings Vancouver attendees shipped by May 29, 2026 — Release Day, the CreativeMornings global CREATE theme activation.

This is not a portfolio gallery. It's a document. Raw, imperfect, real. The medium is the message: here's what this room built in 30 days using AI to externalize their taste and ship something.

---

## Name Options

1. **Sniffin' Glue 2026** (high-commitment; embrace the reference)
2. **Release Day Broadsheet** (descriptive, on-theme)
3. **The Xerox** (the theme we set up in the talk)
4. **You Came** (callback to the closing question)
5. **Both Hands Full** (the core thesis; also a collection of things made while holding the critique)

**Recommendation:** "Sniffin' Glue 2026" if the audience will get the reference. "Release Day Broadsheet" if we want broader accessibility. Decide post-talk based on room energy.

---

## Format

**Format:** Digital (PDF + web), designed to look like a photocopied zine.

**Pages:** Variable, depending on submissions. Target 8-12 pages.

**Visual style:**
- Black and white with optional punk red (#E63B2E) accent
- High contrast, xerox texture
- Hand-lettered feel (can be a font that mimics this)
- Inconsistent layout — intentionally lo-fi, not designed-clean
- No two pages the same grid

**Print option:** Designed so it can be printed on a standard office printer (letter/A4) and folded into a zine format. Stapled, not perfect-bound.

## Content Structure

### Cover
- Title
- "Issue 1 — Creative Mornings Vancouver x BC + AI — May 29, 2026"
- A collage of submission thumbnails (9-16 images)
- Print run: "??? copies" (fill in after shipping)

### Page 1 — Editorial
Short intro (3-4 paragraphs). KK's voice. Connecting the talk to the projects. Something about: this is the thing punk actually was — not the aesthetic, the act. These are the records people made.

### Pages 2-9 (variable) — The Projects

Each project gets a half-page or quarter-page entry:
- Project thumbnail (image, screenshot, or still)
- Title / creator name
- 2-3 sentence description: what they made, what they shipped, what they learned
- QR code or URL if the project is online
- Optional quote from the creator

**Submission format for creators:**
```
1. Your name (how you want to be credited)
2. Project title
3. One image, screenshot, or thumbnail (PNG/JPG, min 800px)
4. 2-3 sentences about what you made and how AI was involved
5. URL if it's online (optional)
6. One sentence you'd want on the zine (optional)
```

**Submission deadline:** May 27, 2026 (2 days before Release Day to allow layout time)
**Submission method:** Google Form (to be created) or email to KK / BC + AI team

### Back Page — Manifesto
Pull the talk's three moves as a short manifesto:
1. Name what you're seeing
2. Externalize your taste
3. Ship something by May 29th

Plus: "Generation is cheap. Taste is not." and "You coming?" (past tense: "You came.")
