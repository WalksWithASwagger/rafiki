# Video Gen Concepts — Punk Rock AI (Creative Mornings Vancouver)

Runway / Kling shot list, concepts, and prompts for promotional video or talk visuals.

---

## Usage Contexts

1. **Social promo** (15-30 sec): Before the talk — drives attendance
2. **Intro reel** (60-90 sec): Plays in the room before KK walks out; sets tone
3. **Talk visual inserts**: Short loops that play behind slides during Act 2 stories
4. **Release Day content**: Behind-the-scenes / B-roll for the May 29 activation

---

## Social Promo (15-30 sec) — "You Coming?"

**Concept:** Fast cut. Archival punk. Modern AI. Voice over from the pitch.

**Shot list:**
1. Xerox machine printing, close-up, paper emerging (2 sec)
2. Cassette tapes stacked (2 sec)
3. DJ hands on turntables, vinyl spinning (2 sec)
4. Punk zine pages flipping, hand-lettered text (2 sec)
5. AI image generation in progress (2 sec)
6. KK's Flickr photos in a grid (2 sec)
7. A crowd at Creative Mornings (2 sec)
8. Text card: "Punk Rock AI — Creative Mornings Vancouver — May 15, 2026"

**VO:** "Corporations build the tools. Weirdos figure out what they're for. You coming?"

**Runway prompt:**
```
A photocopier printing pages, close-up, paper emerging from machine, black and white, documentary style, 1970s aesthetic
```

**Kling prompt:**
```
Close-up shot of a vintage Xerox machine, paper slowly emerging from the feed tray, black and white, high contrast, dramatic lighting, 3 seconds, loop
```

## Intro Reel (60-90 sec) — "The Pattern"

**Concept:** Slow burn through the historical lineage. No talking. Just images and music. The talk's thesis — corporations build, weirdos repurpose — visualized across history.

**Structure:**
- 0:00-0:15 — Dada / cut-up era (Paris, newspaper, scissors, chaos)
- 0:15-0:30 — Kingston sound system (Jamaica, speaker stacks, selector, crowd)
- 0:30-0:45 — UK punk (zines, Xerox, three chords, DIY)
- 0:45-1:00 — Bronx hip-hop (turntables, break, DJ Kool Herc)
- 1:00-1:15 — Cut to: data center (scale, cold, institutional)
- 1:15-1:30 — Cut to: creative people making things with AI (warmth, humanity, craft)

**Music:** Build from lo-fi/ambient to punky energy. Suggest: Death from Above 1979 "Romantic Rights" or Gang of Four "Damaged Goods" — or find royalty-free equivalent.

**Runway / Kling prompt sequence:**

Shot 1 — Dada:
```
1920s Paris cafe, hands cutting newspaper strips with scissors, words falling onto table, black and white, archival film quality, slow motion, artistic chaos
```

Shot 2 — Kingston:
```
1950s outdoor sound system dance, Jamaica, speaker stacks, crowd moving, night, documentary style, warm and electric
```

Shot 3 — Punk:
```
1976 UK, young man at photocopier, stacks of punk fanzines, hand-lettered pages, black and white, underground press
```

Shot 4 — Herc:
```
DJ at two turntables, 1970s basement, vinyl records spinning, hands moving between decks, crowd behind, warm orange light
```

Shot 5 — Data center:
```
hyperscale data center, rows of server racks, cold white light, industrial scale, camera slowly moving through the aisles
```

Shot 6 — Creative AI use:
```
person at desk with screen showing AI creative tool, making something, warm natural light, focused, authentic
```

## Talk Inserts — Act 2 Story Backgrounds

Short loops (10-15 sec, seamless) to play behind Act 2 slides.

```
cassette tapes scattered on a table, dust on the cases, some half-unspooled, warm amber light, slowly rotating camera, nostalgic analog atmosphere, loop

### Luke Minaker (Slide 11)
```
monster creature being sculpted, hands working clay or foam latex, workshop lighting, close-up of the hands and material, craft and creation, loop
```

### Belgium (Slide 12)
```
light changing through a window, slow subtle movement, black and white, quiet, the feeling of waiting and hoping, hospital window aesthetic, loop

```
abstract generative art flowing and shifting, vibrant colors against black, real-time synthesis aesthetic, fluid and dynamic, loop
```

## Technical Notes

- **Runway:** Preferred for photorealistic and archival-aesthetic shots
- **Kling:** Strong for motion loops and abstract generative visuals
- **DALL-E video (when available):** Use for stylized/illustrated content
- **All loops:** 10-15 sec seamless for presentation use; 30 sec for social
- **Export settings:** 1920x1080 minimum; 4K preferred for projection
- **Color treatment:** Match the slide palette — high contrast, selective color, never pastel
