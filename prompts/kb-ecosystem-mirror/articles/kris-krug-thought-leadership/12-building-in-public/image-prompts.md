# Image Prompts — Building In Public Is A Filter For Authenticity

**Status:** Ready

## 1. Hero Image

**For:** Article header / LinkedIn featured image
**Aspect Ratio:** 16:9
**Style:** kk
**Prompt:**
> A studio or workshop with walls made entirely of glass — the work inside fully visible from outside. The artist-builder works without curtains, without secrecy. Observers see in from the dark exterior, but they're not invading — they're witnessing. Dark night outside, warm amber light inside the studio, teal on the work itself at center. The transparency is the strategy, not the vulnerability. Deep dark background (#0f0f1a), warm amber interior (#d97706), teal on the work (#00c8b4). Dark editorial illustration, cinematic.

## 2. Section Image — Stealth Build (Opaque)

**For:** Problem framing section
**Aspect Ratio:** 1:1
**Style:** kk
**Prompt:**
> A figure in a sealed room, alone, working on something hidden in shadow. Doors locked, windows dark, no external light entering. The isolation of secrecy made claustrophobic. Muted purple tones, no warmth, no teal. The self-imposed darkness of building without an audience. Dark editorial illustration.

## 3. Section Image — Public Build (Transparent)

**For:** Contrast section
**Aspect Ratio:** 1:1
**Style:** kk
**Prompt:**
> Same figure, same work — but the walls are glass. Other figures outside watching with genuine curiosity and interest. The work is better, not worse, for being observed. The audience as creative constraint and catalyst. Warm amber interior, teal work, dark exterior with watching silhouettes. Dark editorial illustration.

## 4. Section Image — Authenticity Filter

**For:** Framework section
**Aspect Ratio:** 1:1
**Style:** kk
**Prompt:**
> A filter mechanism at the center of the frame — not a quality filter but a voice filter. Generic, polished, risk-free output on the left approaches and is stopped. Opinionated, specific, voiced output passes through and glows teal. The filter rewards the real thing and stops the performed thing. Teal for what passes, grey-purple for what doesn't. Dark editorial illustration.
