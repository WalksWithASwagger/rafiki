# Data Center Protest Signs — DIY handwritten, "both hands full" / walk-down-the-middle

**Purpose:** Funny, weird, halfway-legible handmade protest signs for a Vancouver data-center
protest. Tone: refuses to pick a side — holds love and critique in the same breath. Same DNA
as "This is the dumbest timeline & I wouldn't miss it."

**Render notes:** style=none. Each prompt bakes in the marker-on-cardboard look. Materials,
colors, and doodles vary on purpose so the set looks like one human made a messy stack — not a
template. Exact sign text is given in quotes; it must be spelled exactly, no extra words.

---

## 1. Dumbest timeline (the keeper)
**Aspect Ratio:** 9:16
**Prompt:**
> Flat front-on photo of a handmade protest sign on a piece of brown corrugated cardboard. Hand-lettered in thick black marker, casual real human handwriting, slightly crooked baseline, uneven letter spacing, a couple of letters cramped at the end of a line where they ran out of room, a few marker smudges and streaks. The sign reads exactly, in capitals across three lines: "THIS IS THE DUMBEST TIMELINE [ampersand] I WOULDN'T MISS IT", where [ampersand] is rendered as a single big hand-drawn ampersand symbol joining the two halves. A wobbly hand-drawn red underline under "WOULDN'T MISS IT". Plain neutral wall background, even lighting. DIY zine energy, readable but imperfect. No logos, no watermark, no extra text.
