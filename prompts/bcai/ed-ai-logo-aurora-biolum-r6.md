# ED + AI Round 6 - Aurora Bioluminescent Afrofuturism

**Goal:** Build a distinct `ED + AI` / `AI Education Meetup` identity under the BC + AI ecosystem that finally has color, life, and point of view: pop Afrofuturism, aurora light, bioluminescent signal, and explicit parent-brand lockup studies.

**Creative correction:** R4 became corporate vector tech. R5 became artifact-like but lost too much of the BC + AI ecosystem and pop futurist energy. R6 should feel like a living sub-brand in the BC + AI family: luminous, magnetic, community-made, future-facing, and event-ready without becoming sponsor-deck bland.

**Parent brand posture:** The attached official BC + AI logo is a parent-brand reference and lockup ingredient. Use it carefully. `ED + AI` remains the hero in every concept. `BC + AI` appears as a small official parent mark, endorsement, or companion lockup only where requested. Do not let the parent logo swallow the ED + AI identity.

**Visual engine:** Aurora ribbons, bioluminescent glow, phosphorescent edge light, pop Afrofuturist color blocking, future-ancestral rhythm, youth worldbuilding, culture-based STEAM, community belonging. Think luminous night garden, underwater signal, community radio, future classroom portal, and festival-quality identity system.

**Palette:** BC + AI evergreen/teal/birch as foundation; aurora cyan, violet, electric teal, spectral green, warm gold, coral, and aubergine as expressive accents. Use glow and contrast, but avoid generic neon cyberpunk.

**Text policy:** Use `ED + AI` as hero text. Use optional `AI Education Meetup`. Use `BC + AI` only as tiny parent mark/credit or in lockup concepts. Generated text may be rough; final production type will be cleaned manually.

**Hard bans:** corporate tech logo, SaaS startup mark, generic innovation summit identity, sponsor-deck blandness, robots, robot teachers, brains, dashboards, generic node networks, hex-grid tech wallpaper, graduation caps, apples, school buses, lightbulbs, fake seals, Ethos name/logo, Atlanthos text, Ubuntu text, copied cultural symbols, kente, adinkra, masks, faces, generic pan-African flag cliche, sterile vector rings, glossy crypto/web3 vibes.
