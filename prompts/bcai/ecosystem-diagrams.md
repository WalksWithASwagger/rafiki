# BC AI Community Centre: Ecosystem Diagrams

Example prompts for visualizing the BC AI Community Centre ecosystem as interconnected mycelial networks.

## 1. Core Ecosystem — Foundational Structure

**For:** Board presentations, investor decks, strategic planning documents
**Aspect:** 16:9
**File name:** bcai-core-ecosystem.png

**Prompt:**
> A comprehensive BC AI ecosystem diagram visualized as a mycelial network. At the center, a large prominent node labeled "Ethics, Compliance, Standards & Social License" serves as the foundational hub. Radiating outward in organic branching patterns are five major nodes: "Data Ecosystem" positioned upper left, "Compute Infrastructure" at upper right, "AI Talent & Training" at middle left, "AI-First Strategies" at middle right, and "AI-Ready Adoption" at lower left. Above these, a larger elevated node represents "AI Leadership" with connections flowing to all major components. Extending from the leadership node, an upward branching path labeled "Voice & Narrative" shows outreach and storytelling initiatives. All connections are organic curves with subtle glowing edges, varying in thickness to show strength of relationships. Node sizes reflect importance and centrality within the ecosystem. The overall layout suggests natural growth from a strong ethical foundation upward through infrastructure and capability development toward leadership and external communication.

## 3. Data & Compute Infrastructure — Technical Flow

**For:** Technical strategy documents, infrastructure planning, capacity analysis
**Aspect:** 16:9
**File name:** bcai-infrastructure-flow.png

**Prompt:**
> A technical infrastructure diagram showing BC AI's data and compute ecosystem as interconnected mycelial pathways. Bottom layer shows "Data Sources" as multiple small nodes representing open datasets, private datasets, federated learning sources, community data initiatives, and research datasets. These connect upward through organic branching paths to a middle layer of "Data Processing & Governance" showing nodes for data cleaning, privacy protection, ethical review, and quality assurance. From there, connections flow to "Compute Resources" layer showing distributed compute nodes of varying sizes representing cloud infrastructure, on-premise servers, edge computing, and specialized AI hardware. At the top, "AI Models & Applications" node receives processed data through compute infrastructure. Bidirectional flow arrows show data flowing up through the system and insights/models flowing back down for refinement. Side branches show "Access Control & Security" nodes ensuring protected pathways. Include capacity indicators showing where infrastructure is robust (thick, bright connections) versus where expansion is needed (thinner, aspirational dotted connections). The overall diagram suggests a living, breathing technical ecosystem with natural flow patterns and growth potential.

## 5. Innovation Ecosystem — From Idea to Impact

**For:** Innovation strategy, startup support programs, commercialization pathways
**Aspect:** 1:1
**File name:** bcai-innovation-ecosystem.png

**Prompt:**
> BC AI innovation ecosystem showing the journey from idea to impact as organic growth pattern. Center shows "Research & Discovery" node representing foundational research at universities and labs. From there, branching paths lead to "Proof of Concept" nodes showing early-stage validation. These connect to "Startup Formation" region showing incubators, accelerators, and early funding sources as a cluster of medium nodes. Parallel branch shows "Industry R&D" for innovation within established companies. Both pathways converge toward "Scale & Growth" region showing venture capital, scale-up programs, and market expansion support. Throughout, show support infrastructure nodes: mentorship networks, technical resources, business development support, regulatory guidance, and ethical review. Include "Community Impact Assessment" nodes ensuring innovations serve community needs. Success pathways show connections to "Market Deployment" and "Social Benefit" as dual outcomes. Show that not all paths are linear - include feedback loops where market learnings inform research, where failed startups seed new ideas, where industry challenges inspire academic research. The overall structure emphasizes an ecosystem that supports innovation from spark to impact while maintaining ethical grounding and community benefit focus.

## 6. Regional Network — BC's AI Geography

**For:** Regional development plans, investment attraction, provincial strategy
**Aspect:** 16:9
**File name:** bcai-regional-network.png

**Prompt:**
> A geographical network diagram showing BC AI activity distributed across British Columbia as interconnected regions. Center-left shows "Metro Vancouver" as the largest node cluster with sub-nodes for downtown tech hub, university research centers, startup districts, and corporate R&D facilities. Connected to it via strong pathways are "Victoria" (medium node showing government, tech scene, and university research), "Kelowna" (growing node showing emerging tech ecosystem), "Prince George" (node representing northern innovation and resource sector AI), "Kamloops" (node for interior initiatives), and several Indigenous community nodes showing distributed presence across the province. Coastal connections show "Coastal First Nations" innovation initiatives. Each regional node has internal structure showing local ecosystem components (education, industry, government, community). Connections between regions show knowledge sharing, talent mobility, partnership networks, and resource sharing. Show how provincial infrastructure (fiber networks, compute resources, funding programs) connects all regions as supportive mycelial base. Include aspirational connections showing where network growth is planned. The overall diagram emphasizes BC AI as truly provincial - not just Vancouver-centric but distributed across the province with each region contributing unique strengths while connected to the whole.

## 7. Ethical AI Framework — Values in Action

**For:** Governance documents, ethical framework presentations, values communication
**Aspect:** 1:1
**File name:** bcai-ethical-framework.png

**Prompt:**
> BC AI's ethical framework visualized as a mycelial root system supporting the entire ecosystem. Deep center shows interconnected foundational values as root nodes: "Indigenous Data Sovereignty," "Privacy & Security," "Transparency & Explainability," "Fairness & Non-Discrimination," "Environmental Sustainability," "Community Benefit," and "Democratic Governance." These roots connect upward to "Standards & Compliance" layer showing policy frameworks, regulatory alignment, certification programs, and audit processes. From there, connections flow to "Implementation Practices" nodes showing how ethics manifest in actual work: responsible data collection, bias testing, stakeholder consultation, environmental impact assessment, benefit measurement, and inclusive design. At the top layer, "Accountability Mechanisms" nodes show oversight bodies, community advisory boards, ethical review processes, and public reporting. Throughout the network, show feedback loops where community input influences standards, where implementation learnings update practices, where accountability findings adjust policies. Include indicators of maturity - some pathways well-established (thick, solid), others emerging (thinner, developing), some aspirational (dotted, planned). The overall structure emphasizes ethics not as constraint but as foundation - the mycelial network that nourishes and sustains the entire BC AI ecosystem, connecting all parts to shared values and accountable practices.
