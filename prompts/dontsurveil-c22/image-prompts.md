# dontsurveil.me — Bill C-22 page image prompts

Source page: https://opencivics-labs.github.io/dontsurveil-me/c22.html
Aesthetic: matches the page — black, white, blood-red, minimal, urgent, editorial.
Style: `zine` (black/white/blood-red xerox-grain collage — resistance zine aesthetic).

---

## 1. Paradigm Shift — Option A (key duplication)
**For:** Section "The paradigm shift", after the Today vs. C-22 comparison table
**Aspect Ratio:** 16:9
**Prompt:**
> A single brass key on the left half of the frame, sharply lit, stark and isolated. On the right half, the same key duplicated three times in a fan, the duplicates rendered in flat blood-red ink as if stamped through a photocopier. A thin red dividing line splits the two halves. High-contrast black background, heavy xerox grain, no text, no logos. Editorial protest-poster composition.

## 2. Paradigm Shift — Option B (padlock with hidden master)
**For:** Section "The paradigm shift", after the Today vs. C-22 comparison table
**Aspect Ratio:** 16:9
**Prompt:**
> A heavy industrial padlock photographed straight-on in stark black and white, its keyhole glowing faint red. Behind the lock, a second silhouette of the same lock offset by a few pixels in blood-red ink, suggesting a duplicate. Distressed photocopy texture, halftone dot noise across the background. Stripped, urgent, no text.

## 3. Why This Is About You — Option A (eight phones, one eye)
**For:** Section "Why this is about you", below the eight use-case bullets
**Aspect Ratio:** 16:9
**Prompt:**
> A grid of eight smartphone silhouettes arranged like a contact sheet, each screen glowing with a different intimate text-message fragment rendered as abstract scribbles, no readable words. A large red open eye is collaged across the entire grid, semi-transparent, ink-stamped, watching all eight. Black background, white phones, blood-red eye and accents. Xerox grain, protest zine aesthetic, no logos.

## 4. Why This Is About You — Option B (chorus of silhouettes)
**For:** Section "Why this is about you", below the eight use-case bullets
**Aspect Ratio:** 16:9
**Prompt:**
> Eight diverse human silhouettes in profile, shoulder to shoulder, each holding a phone to their ear or in front of their face — a parent, a doctor, a lawyer, a journalist, an organizer, a small-business owner, a traveler crossing a border, a person seeking help. All rendered in flat black on white with heavy halftone dot texture. A single blood-red speech bubble overlaps the entire row, fragmenting across each figure. Editorial zine poster, no text, no logos.

## 7. If This Passes — Option A (three smoldering breaches)
**For:** Section "If this passes", below the four-stage rollout (sets up the "It has happened before" callout)
**Aspect Ratio:** 16:9
**Prompt:**
> Three identical broken padlocks arranged in a horizontal row across a stark white background, each rendered in flat black ink with thin red cracks splintering across them. Wisps of red smoke rise from each lock and merge into a single column of smoke at the top of the frame. Heavy photocopy distortion, halftone shadow, zine collage texture. Stark, mournful, protest poster framing, no text.
